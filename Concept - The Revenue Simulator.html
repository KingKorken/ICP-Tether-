E-Credits Revenue Formula
Revenue = (Total kWh × Hourly RES-E% × 4) × (Avoided CO2/kWh × CO2 Price)

Avoided CO2 = 338.4g (fossil baseline) − 15g (grid CI) = 323.4g/kWh

The 4x multiplier comes from EU RED III: every 1 kWh of renewable electricity delivered to an EV counts as 4 kWh toward the GHG reduction target. Sweden's Reduktionsplikt (raised to 10%, July 2025) now accepts these electricity credits.

Grid Flexibility Revenue Formula
Monthly Revenue = MW_Bid × Price_per_MW × Hours_in_Month

MW_Bid = Chargers × Utilization × MW_per_charger × Flex_Potential

Markets stacked: mFRR (30% UP / 70% DOWN) + FCR-D (30% UP / 70% DOWN)

Tether stacks revenue across FCR and mFRR markets simultaneously. Revenue split: CPO 40% / Tether 40% / BRP-BSP 10%.

Full Problem Analysis

Charge Point Operators in Europe face a fundamental trust problem when evaluating flexibility and e-credit revenue partners. Public DC charging networks report single-digit average utilization (~8%), creating intense pressure to find additional revenue streams. Yet the EV charging industry has seen exits and bankruptcies, with charger reliability rates at only 64-80% — making CPOs deeply skeptical of startup revenue projections.

When CPOs evaluate services like Tether's, they receive revenue estimates from multiple vendors, each claiming “market data” but none showing methodology. The result: promising partnerships stall because CPOs can't build an internal business case around a number they can't explain to their own board.

The dual-stream opportunity (passive e-credits + active grid flexibility) compounds this problem. No existing tool shows both revenue streams together, and none show the temporal dimension — how revenue builds over time and what each month of delay costs.

Competitive Landscape

  • Competitor PowerFlex — 7 inputs, visual output, 10-year bar chart. No flexibility revenue, no methodology shown.
  • Competitor Sympower — 4 inputs, email-gated, single figure. Targets heavy industry, not CPOs.
  • Competitor Voltus — Minimal friction, bold headline. No transparency on methodology.
  • Competitor Stable Auto (Evaluate) — ML-powered, 70+ variables. Paid product, too complex for lead magnet.
  • Competitor GridBeyond — 3 inputs, Revenue + Savings split. Industrial/commercial focus, not CPOs.
