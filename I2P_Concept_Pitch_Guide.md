# Deliverables #1 Guide — Concept Pitch Page (I2P)

## Overview

Each team must deliver **a minimum of 3 Concept Pitch Pages** (1 for each solution you are imagining for your client). The goal is to explore different directions, not fall in love with the most obvious solution.

These pages are both your **Deliverable #1 for I2P** and your **Pitch to the Stakeholder**.

- **For Pitch to Stakeholder** — Prioritize this. Prepare your pitch in a user-friendly format to present your potential solutions to the client.
- **For I2P Deliverable** — Submit a PDF file (exported from any software) to Moodle by Monday. If you need an extra day or have doubts, ask your mentor.

---

## Guidelines

### Content Rules

- Write for someone who knows nothing about your project.
- Lead with the problem, not the solution.
- Every section should earn its space — if it doesn't add clarity, cut it.
- Use real data and references wherever possible.

### Concept Selection Rules

- Minimum **3 potential solution concepts** per team.
- At least **1 concept** can be a direct, pragmatic answer to the client brief.
- The others must **push beyond the brief** — creative, innovative, or exploring future client needs.
- Each concept is its own **standalone page** — don't reference other concepts.

### Format Rules

- If your client has brand guidelines (logo, colors, fonts), apply them. If not, keep it clean and professional. **Clarity above form.**
- Each Concept Pitch Page should take **under 3 minutes to read** and be fully understandable without a verbal presentation.
- **No wireframes or UI mockups** — this is about the idea, not the execution.
- Diagrams, illustrations, and simple visuals are welcome.
- The deliverable format is **PDF**, but the client presentation can be done in any format (Notion, Google Slides, PPT, HTML) and then exported to PDF.

## Concept Pitch Page Sections

### 1. Header

- **Concept name** — Give it a memorable, descriptive name.
- **One-line tagline** — What is this in one sentence?
- **Team name + Client name/logo**
- **Date**

### 2. The Problem

2–3 sentences maximum. What pain point, gap, or opportunity does this concept address? Be specific. Use data or quotes from client conversations if you have them.

> ⚠️ This is NOT a restatement of the brief. This is your team's framing of the real root problem behind the brief. Show that you have been digging and asking "Why?" many times.

### 3. Research & Insights

What did you find that shaped this concept? This section builds credibility and shows depth.

Include:

- Key findings from your research (market, user, competitor, technology)
- Relevant data points or statistics
- Quotes or observations from client/user conversations
- Links to sources inline where relevant

Keep it tight — **3 to 5 key insights**, not a research dump. Save the full research for the Appendix.

### 4. The Concept

What is the potential solution? Explain it clearly enough that someone with zero context understands what you're proposing.

Cover:

- **What it is** — Describe the product/tool/system in plain language.
- **Who it's for** — Primary user(s).
- **How it works** — A simple 3–5 step user journey or flow (use text, diagrams, or illustrations — no wireframes or mockups needed at this stage).
- **What makes it different** — Why this approach vs. the obvious one.

### 5. Why This Matters

Connect the concept back to the client's world. Why should they care about this direction?

Include the **Concept Scorecard** — three dimensions, each scored 1 (lowest) to 5 (highest) by your team:

| Dimension | Score (1–5) | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **Impact** | | How much value does this create for the client's business or users? |
| **Innovation** | | How differentiated or creative is this vs. the obvious solution? |
| **Feasibility** | | Can this be realistically delivered within the I2P timeline? |

Be honest. Concept 1 (the safe answer) might score 5 on feasibility and 2 on innovation. That's fine — the scorecard exists to make trade-offs visible.

### 6. Next Steps

What needs to happen to validate or move forward with this concept? Be specific:

- What assumptions need testing?
- What information is still missing?
- What would the first prototype look like?
- What decisions does the client need to make?

### 7. Appendix

Organized reference section. Include anything that supports the pitch but doesn't belong in the main flow:

- Full research links (competitors, articles, reports, frameworks)
- Detailed data tables or analysis
- Technology references (platforms, APIs, tools considered)
- Inspiration and moodboard references
- Any additional context the client team might want to explore

Organize with clear sub-headings. Label your links — don't just paste URLs.

---

## Checklist Before Submitting

- [ ] Can someone with zero context understand the concept in under 3 minutes?
- [ ] Is the problem framed in your own words (not copy-pasted from the brief)?
- [ ] Does the research section have real insights with sources?
- [ ] Is the user journey or flow clear without wireframes?
- [ ] Are the 3 scores filled in with honest rationale?
- [ ] Are next steps specific and actionable?
- [ ] Is the appendix organized with labeled links?
