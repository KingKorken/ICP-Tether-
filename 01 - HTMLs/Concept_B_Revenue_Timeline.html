Full Problem Analysis

When CPOs evaluate new revenue opportunities, they typically receive a single annual projection — "EUR 300K/year." This number feels abstract and disconnected from operational reality. It provides no insight into when revenue starts flowing, how it builds over time, how seasonal dynamics affect it, or what the cost of each month of delay actually is.

The result is perpetual deferral. A static number makes it easy to say "interesting, we'll revisit next quarter." The cost of waiting is invisible, so there's no urgency to act. Revenue opportunities that should close in weeks take months — or never close at all.

Behavioral Economics

  • Research Kahneman, D. & Tversky, A. (1979). "Prospect Theory: An Analysis of Decision under Risk." Econometrica, 47(2). Loss aversion ratio approximately 2:1.
  • Research Thaler, R. (1980). "Toward a Positive Theory of Consumer Choice." Journal of Economic Behavior and Organization, 1(1). Endowment effect and status quo bias.
  • Finding Loss aversion validated in B2B procurement: cost-of-inaction scenarios rated as more decision-relevant than gain scenarios.

Market Data

  • Data Mimer (Svenska kraftnat) — Hourly FCR and mFRR price data for Sweden. Shows strong seasonal variation: winter peaks 2-3x above annual average.
  • Market Nordic mFRR Energy Activation Market — Launched March 2025, immediately produced EUR 2,000/MWh peak imbalance prices.
  • Regulation Swedish Reduktionsplikt — Raised to 10% (July 2025), now accepts electricity credits (elkrediter).
  • Report EY/Eurelectric "Plugging into Potential" (2025) — EUR 4 billion in annual savings from EV flexibility for European grid operators.

Seasonal Revenue Pattern (Sweden, 2025)

Peak months (Nov-Feb): Grid balancing prices spike 2-3x above annual average due to heating demand. Highest flexibility revenue.

Shoulder months (Mar-May, Sep-Oct): Moderate prices. Steady baseline revenue from both streams.

Trough months (Jun-Aug): Lower flexibility prices, but e-credit revenue remains stable. Net annual revenue is 15-25% higher than a flat average would suggest.

Revenue Model

Grid Flexibility (monthly):
Revenue = MW_Bid x Avg_Monthly_Price x Hours_in_Month
MW_Bid = Chargers x Utilization x MW_per_charger x Flex_potential

E-Credits (monthly):
Revenue = Monthly_kWh x RES-E% x 4 x Avoided_CO2 x CO2_Price

Revenue split: CPO 40% / Tether 40% / BRP-BSP 10%
